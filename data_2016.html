Season 1 Analytics

Concluding our first-ever EDH Draft League, this page offers insights and analytics on data that was collected during and after the season. Our draft league was directly inspired by Sheldon Menery's EDH Rotisserie Draft. For those that didn't participate in our league we have a brief rules page, as well as a quick run-down:

The EDH Draft League is a running weekly event during a "season". A group of players began the league by drafting colors to form an exclusive three-color shard/wedge identity. Afterwards, players drafted a pool of three possible commanders in their identity. Following that, the actual draft began. This website was created to facilitate the asynchronous drafting of 768 cards over a two-week period. Each player drafted a pool of 96 cards from all EDH legal cards in existence; proxies were made for unowned cards. Picks were zero-sum; there was only a single copy of each card to be chosen. Once the draft was complete, we played weekly two-game nights using a points system in four-player pods. Players earned points for kills, winning the game, and for votes. Each player voted for their favorite play of the game or best player at the end of each match. Points were averaged over games played; roughly twenty-five matches over nine weeks.

I've removed names in favor of keeping everyone as anonymous as possible, so players are referred to by their commander. Our playgroup consists of mostly casual players with a good mix of all three player profiles. Here are the final average points-per-game and total points for each player, in order of place finish.

  1. Derevi Enchantress - 3.646 PPG / 87.5 total. Winner!
  2. Mimeoplasm Combo - 2.991 PPG / 56.8 total
  3. Alesha Stoneforger - 2.953 PPG / 73.8 total
  4. Hazezon Tamar Swarm - 2.933 PPG / 73.3 total
  5. Karador Combo - 2.929 PPG / 76.2 total
  6. Nekusar Wheels - 2.707 PPG / 67.7 total
  7. Maelstrom Wanderer Combo - 2.278 PPG / 61.5 total
  8. Zur Combo-Control - 1.739 PPG / 40.0 total

Colors Drafted

Breakdown of all cards drafted by color. Color was derived from converted cost with the exception of suspend cards which were adjusted manually. Gold cards were grouped together as a whole to avoid bloating the graph with combinations.


Types Drafted

Breakdown of all cards drafted by card type. Only super-types were counted, and "tribal" was excluded. Multiple supertypes were grouped accordingly:

  • All creature types were grouped under Creature. This includes Artifact Creatures, etc.
  • The Artifacts category was reserved for pure artifacts.
  • Enchantments category included Enchantment Artifacts, but not Enchantment Creatures.

Converted Cost

Total number of drafted cards by converted mana cost. Lands were excluded. Split cards were categorized by total CMC. The highlander award goes to Hazezon for drafting the only 11 CMC spell, Worldspine Wurm.

Aggregate costs in USD of paper versions of everyone's drafted pool. Prices were rounded up to the nearest dollar. Zur's deck cost over $1,000 more than the second place deck! Survey Data

After the league ended we sent out a survey form to be filled out by players anonymously. This gave us some additional insight into how the experience was perceived by everyone. Before diving into the visualizations, here are some high-level take-aways:

  • Vast majority of players were generally happy with the league, their deck, and the draft. Yay!
  • Only one person changed commanders in the entire season (Zur to Oloro)
  • Most people were ambivalent about sideboard rules, and opted for a waiver wire pick each week.
  • Most Surprising Deck: Alesha Stoneforger with 50% majority
  • Crowd Favorite Deck: Alesha Stoneforger with 37.5% majority
  • Least Favorite Deck: Derevi Bantchantress with 62.5% majority
  • There wasn't a single favorite card, but Omniscience took the cake as least favorite!
  • Season length, pod size, and matchup schedule were all well liked by majority
