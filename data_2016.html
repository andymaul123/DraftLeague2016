2016 Draft Post-Mortem

Concluding our first-ever EDH Draft League, this page offers insights and analytics on data that was collected during and after the league. The idea was based on Sheldon's EDH Rotisserie Draft. For those not aware of what the EDH Draft League is, here's a quick run-down:

The EDH Draft League is a running weekly event during a "season". A group of players began the league by drafting colors to form a three-color shard or wedge identity. Afterwards, players drafted a pool of three possible commanders in their identity. Following that the actual draft began. This website was created to facilitate the asynchronous drafting of 768 cards over a two-week period. Each player drafted a pool of 96 cards from all EDH legal cards in existence; proxies were made for unowned cards. Picks were zero-sum; there was only a single copy of each card to be chosen. Once the draft was complete, we played weekly two-game nights using a points system in four-player pods. Players earned points for kills, winning the game, and for votes. Each player voted for their favorite play of the game or best player at the end of each match. Points were averaged over games played; twenty-five matches over nine weeks.

Here are the final average points-per-game and total points for each player:

  1. Michael (Derevi Bantchantress) - 3.646 PPG / 87.5 total
  2. Zack (Mimeoplasm Combo) - 2.991 PPG / 56.8 total
  3. Andy (Alesha Stoneforger) - 2.953 PPG / 73.8 total
  4. Brendan (Hazezon Tamar Swarm) - 2.933 PPG / 73.3 total
  5. Justin (Karador Combo) - 2.929 PPG / 76.2 total
  6. Hopper (Nekusar Wheels) - 2.707 PPG / 67.7 total
  7. Ryan (Maelstrom Wanderer Combo) - 2.278 PPG / 61.5 total
  8. Blake (Zur Enchantress) - 1.739 PPG / 40.0 total

Colors Drafted

Breakdown of all cards drafted by color. Color was derived from converted cost with the exception of suspend cards which were adjusted manually. Gold cards were grouped together as a whole to avoid bloating the graph with combinations.


Types Drafted

Breakdown of all cards drafted by card type. Only super-types were counted, and "tribal" was excluded. Multiple supertypes were grouped accordingly:

  • All creature types were grouped under Creature. This includes Artifact Creatures, etc.
  • The Artifacts category was reserved for pure artifacts.
  • Enchantments category included Enchantment Artifacts, but not Enchantment Creatures.

Converted Cost

Total number of drafted cards by converted mana cost. Lands were excluded. Split cards were categorized by total CMC. The highlander award goes to Brendan for drafting the only 11 CMC spell, Worldspine Wurm.


Deck Prices

Aggregate costs in USD of paper versions of everyone's drafted pool. Prices were rounded up to the nearest dollar.
